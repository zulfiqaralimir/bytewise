# Module 80 — Regulation for Investment Managers

Investment managers operate within a web of regulations that varies by fund domicile, manager location, investor location, and asset class. Getting regulation wrong is existential — an unregistered fund or an undisclosed regulatory breach can result in mandatory wind-down, fines, and personal liability. This module covers the regulatory framework for a Pakistan/Gulf-focused investment manager building FERROQUANT Capital.

## Learning Objectives

- Navigate SECP's regulatory framework for non-bank financial institutions and asset managers
- Understand AIFMD requirements for marketing to European investors
- Know the SEC registration thresholds and exemptions relevant to a non-US manager
- Implement FATCA and CRS obligations for a fund accepting international investors
- Build a practical compliance program appropriate for FERROQUANT's size and strategy
- Design the regulatory structure for FERROQUANT's planned expansion into DIFC

## 1. Pakistan Regulatory Framework

### SECP — Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan

SECP is Pakistan's primary capital markets regulator, established under the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan Act 1997. SECP regulates:
- Capital markets (PSX, bond markets)
- Non-bank financial companies (NBFCs)
- Insurance companies
- Pension funds
- Mutual funds and collective investment schemes
- Listed companies (continuous disclosure, corporate governance)

**SECP's mandate for investment managers:**
Any entity managing third-party capital in Pakistan must be licensed by SECP under the Non-Banking Finance Companies (NBFC) Regulations 2008 or the NBFC and Notified Entities Regulations 2008.

### SECP NBFC Licensing for Investment Managers

**License categories relevant to FERROQUANT:**

| Category | Activity | Minimum Capital |
|---|---|---|
| Investment Finance Services | Discretionary and non-discretionary portfolio management | PKR 200M |
| Asset Management Company (AMC) | Managing open-end or closed-end mutual funds | PKR 200M |
| Investment Advisor | Providing investment advice for compensation | PKR 10M |

**FERROQUANT's path:**
1. **Phase 1 (early stage):** Investment Advisor license (lower capital requirement, less restrictive)
2. **Phase 2 (scale):** Investment Finance Services license for discretionary portfolio management
3. **Phase 3 (institutional):** Consider AMC license if launching a regulated collective investment scheme (CIS)

**Licensing requirements (Investment Finance Services):**
- Minimum paid-up capital: PKR 200 million
- Fit and proper criteria for directors and key executives (SECP background check)
- Minimum two qualified (CFA/CA/MBA Finance) professional staff
- Compliance officer and risk officer required
- SBP approval required if managing foreign investors' funds (FX regulations)
- Annual renewal with audited accounts submission to SECP

### NBFC Regulations — Key Ongoing Requirements

**Capital adequacy:**
Maintain minimum capital adequacy ratio (CAR) as specified by SECP. FERROQUANT must ensure fund management fees and operations do not erode the management company's capital below the minimum.

**Reporting:**
- Quarterly SECP returns: AUM, portfolio composition, investor summary
- Annual audited accounts within 4 months of year-end
- Material events: notify SECP within 24 hours (regulatory inquiry, key person departure, significant loss)

**Investment restrictions (for SECP-registered funds):**
- No single issuer exposure > 10% of fund NAV (for diversified funds)
- No investment in related-party securities without board approval
- Derivatives only for hedging purposes (not speculation) in most SECP fund structures
- Leverage limits: typically 1:1 for regulated funds

**Note:** These restrictions apply to formally regulated CIS (mutual funds). Privately placed funds (similar to hedge funds) have more flexibility but still require NBFC licensing.

### SBP Requirements for FX

Any investment manager accepting foreign capital or investing in foreign assets requires SBP approval:

**Repatriation:** Foreign investors must be able to repatriate profits and capital. The SBP's FE Regulations 2002 govern repatriation. FERROQUANT must ensure its fund structure allows compliant repatriation.

**NRP accounts:** Non-Resident Pakistanis (NRPs) can invest through NRP Roshan Digital Accounts — FERROQUANT can accept NRP investments through this channel.

**Foreign exchange risk:** If FERROQUANT holds USD cash or invests in USD-denominated instruments, SBP FX permissions may be required. Consult specialized counsel before accepting USD-denominated foreign investment.

## 2. AIFMD — Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (EU)

### What AIFMD Is

AIFMD (2011, revised AIFMD II 2024) is the EU's regulatory framework for Alternative Investment Fund Managers (AIFMs). It applies to:
- Any manager of an Alternative Investment Fund (AIF) — hedge funds, PE funds, real estate funds
- With EU investors OR managing EU assets OR marketing to EU investors

**FERROQUANT's AIFMD exposure:** If FERROQUANT ever markets to European institutional investors (e.g., a Dutch pension fund, German insurance company), AIFMD applies.

### AIFMD Authorization vs Registration

**Full AIFMD Authorization:**
Required if AUM > EUR 500M (or > EUR 100M with leverage). Requires:
- Regulatory authorization from EU member state regulator (e.g., Luxembourg CSSF, Ireland Central Bank)
- Depositary requirement: an EU-regulated depositary must hold fund assets and perform oversight
- Annual Annex IV reporting to national regulators
- Full AIFMD disclosure to investors

**Registration (lighter regime):**
For managers below the EUR 100–500M thresholds. Registration (not full authorization) with lower compliance burden.

**National Private Placement Regime (NPPR):**
Non-EU managers (like FERROQUANT) can market to EU professional investors under each EU country's NPPR — without full AIFMD authorization. Requirements vary by country but typically include:
- Registration with the relevant national regulator
- Annual disclosure filings
- Compliance with AIFMD disclosure requirements to investors

**Practical path for FERROQUANT:** Use NPPR to access UK (post-Brexit: FCA's NPPR/Overseas Funds Regime) and key EU markets (Netherlands, Luxembourg, Germany). Full AIFMD authorization only necessary if EU AUM exceeds the threshold or if targeting retail investors.

### Key AIFMD Requirements

**Annex IV Reporting:**
Quarterly/semi-annual reporting to regulators covering:
- Portfolio composition, leverage, geographical exposure
- Main instruments traded, principal markets
- Liquidity profile, redemption terms
- Risk profile: VaR, stress test results

**Depositary:**
A regulated depositary (major bank) must:
- Hold the fund's assets (or verify title for non-custodied assets)
- Monitor cash flows
- Oversee fund administration compliance

This is a significant operational requirement — FERROQUANT would need a European depositary for any EU-authorized structure.

**Remuneration:**
AIFMD regulates performance fee structures to prevent excessive risk-taking:
- Performance fees must be aligned with long-term investor interests
- HWM requirement for performance fees
- Deferral of variable compensation for senior staff (typically 3–5 years)

## 3. SEC Registration — United States

### Investment Advisers Act Registration

US investment adviser registration is triggered by:
- Having US clients (investors or advisory clients)
- Exceeding AUM thresholds
- Using US interstate commerce to provide advice

**Registration thresholds:**

| AUM | Requirement |
|---|---|
| < USD 25M | State-registered (if any US clients) |
| USD 25M – USD 110M | State or SEC registered (depends on state) |
| > USD 110M | SEC-registered (federal) |
| Exempt | Private fund adviser exemption (if qualifying) |

**Private fund adviser exemption (Rule 204(a)-1):**
Non-US advisers with:
- No US place of business
- All US clients are "qualified purchasers" (QPs — USD 25M+ investable assets)
- No public solicitation in the US

May be exempt from SEC registration even with US clients. File Form ADV Part 1 as an "exempt reporting adviser" (ERA).

**3(c)(1) and 3(c)(7) fund exemptions:**
Investment funds seeking to avoid being regulated as "investment companies" under the Investment Company Act use these exemptions:
- **3(c)(1):** No more than 100 beneficial owners; any accredited investors
- **3(c)(7):** Unlimited beneficial owners; but ALL must be "qualified purchasers" (USD 25M+ net worth)

**FERROQUANT US strategy:** Accept US investors only if they are qualified purchasers. Structure as 3(c)(7) fund. File as an ERA (exempt reporting adviser). No SEC registration required unless establishing a US office.

## 4. FATCA & CRS

### FATCA — Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (US)

FATCA (2010) requires non-US financial institutions to identify and report US persons' accounts to the IRS (directly or through their country's tax authority under an IGA — Intergovernmental Agreement).

**Pakistan-US IGA:** Pakistan has signed a Model 1B IGA with the US. FERROQUANT, as a Pakistani NBFC, is a Foreign Financial Institution (FFI) under FATCA.

**FERROQUANT's FATCA obligations:**
1. **Register** with IRS as an FFI, obtain GIIN (Global Intermediary Identification Number)
2. **Due diligence:** Identify US persons among investors (W-9 for US persons, W-8BEN for non-US)
3. **Reporting:** Report US investors' account balances, income, and capital gains annually to FBR (Federal Board of Revenue), which transmits to IRS under IGA
4. **Withholding:** Withhold 30% on certain US-source payments to non-compliant investors

**Practical impact:** FERROQUANT must collect tax forms from every investor and annually report US-person investors. Not onerous for a fund with a limited investor base, but requires a systematic process.

### CRS — Common Reporting Standard (OECD)

CRS is the global equivalent of FATCA — an OECD framework where 100+ countries share financial account information with each other's tax authorities.

**Pakistan CRS participation:** Pakistan is a CRS signatory. FERROQUANT must:
1. Identify each investor's country of tax residence
2. Report relevant account information (balance, income) to FBR
3. FBR transmits to the investor's home country tax authority

**Practical impact for FERROQUANT:**
- Collect self-certification from every investor (their tax residence, TIN)
- Collect W-8BEN/W-9 forms for FATCA, self-certification for CRS
- Build annual reporting process to FBR
- Hire a tax counsel familiar with both FATCA and CRS

**Gulf investor CRS:** UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait are all CRS signatories. FERROQUANT's Gulf investors will be reported to their home country tax authorities. Ensure Gulf investors are aware of this — it is sometimes a sensitive disclosure issue.

## 5. DIFC Regulatory Framework

### Why DIFC Matters for FERROQUANT

The Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) is a financial free zone with its own legal system (English common law based) and regulator (DFSA — Dubai Financial Services Authority). It is the premier location for investment managers targeting Gulf institutional capital.

**Key advantages of DIFC:**
- Recognized by Gulf SWFs as a credible jurisdiction
- DFSA authorization adds significant credibility
- Access to DIFC courts (English common law, enforceable globally)
- English language documentation and proceedings
- 0% corporate tax (outside UAE's 9% corporate tax, which applies to DIFC entities differently)
- Strong talent pool of finance professionals

### DFSA Authorization Categories

| Category | Activity | Minimum Capital |
|---|---|---|
| Category 3C | Managing a Collective Investment Fund | USD 250,000 |
| Category 3A | Managing Assets (discretionary) | USD 500,000 |
| Category 4 | Advising on Financial Products | USD 10,000 |

**FERROQUANT DIFC path:**
1. Incorporate a DIFC entity (DIFC Limited Company)
2. Apply for DFSA Category 3C or 3A license
3. The DIFC entity markets to Gulf investors; Pakistan entity manages the investments
4. Structure allows compliance with both SECP (Pakistan operations) and DFSA (Gulf marketing)

**DFSA requirements:**
- Minimum capital per category
- Fit and proper assessment for key persons (Senior Executive Officer, Finance Officer, Compliance Officer, MLRO)
- Compliance manual, risk management framework, AML/CFT policies
- Annual DFSA fees (USD 15,000–25,000/year for typical hedge fund)
- Annual audit by DFSA-registered auditor

### ADGM — Abu Dhabi Global Market

ADGM is Abu Dhabi's competing financial free zone (similar to DIFC but regulated by FSRA — Financial Services Regulatory Authority).

**ADGM vs DIFC for FERROQUANT:**

| Factor | DIFC | ADGM |
|---|---|---|
| Established | 2004 (more established) | 2015 (newer, growing) |
| Tenant quality | Goldman Sachs, HSBC, JP Morgan | Hub71 tech focus, growing financial |
| Regulator | DFSA (strong reputation) | FSRA (growing reputation) |
| SWF access | ADIA (Dubai) accessible | Mubadala, ADIC (Abu Dhabi) accessible |
| Cost | Slightly higher | Competitive |
| Recommendation | Better for established managers | Good for tech/startup-adjacent funds |

**Recommendation for FERROQUANT:** DIFC first (stronger brand recognition for quant fund), consider dual presence later if targeting Abu Dhabi-specific SWFs.

## 6. Building a Compliance Program

### Compliance Program Components

A compliance program is not just a document — it is a living system that prevents regulatory violations before they happen.

**Core components:**

**1. Compliance Policies and Procedures Manual**
Documents exactly how the firm complies with each applicable regulation. Should cover:
- Trading policies (personal account dealing, restricted list, insider information)
- Marketing policies (what can be said to investors, approved materials)
- Conflicts of interest (identification, disclosure, management)
- AML/CFT procedures (KYC, due diligence, STR filing)
- Data protection (investor data handling)
- Business continuity

**2. Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)**
The CCO has personal responsibility for the firm's compliance. Must be:
- Independent of portfolio management (cannot be also the portfolio manager)
- Senior enough to raise issues at the highest level
- Knowledgeable about applicable regulations

**3. Trade Surveillance**
Automated monitoring of all trades against:
- Restricted list (securities where firm has MNPI)
- Personal account dealing policy violations
- Front-running patterns (trading ahead of client orders)
- Position limit breaches
- Unusual trading patterns (insider trading flags)

**4. Personal Account Dealing Policy**
Investment professionals must disclose personal holdings and trades. Standard rules:
- Pre-clearance required for trades in securities covered by firm's research
- Blackout periods around fund trading in the same securities
- Quarterly portfolio disclosure to CCO
- 30-day holding period (trades reversed within 30 days require compliance review)

**5. Annual Compliance Review**
Full assessment of:
- Were all policies followed?
- Were there any regulatory inquiries or complaints?
- Any new regulations requiring policy updates?
- Training completed by all staff?

### AML/CFT for Investment Managers

Investment managers are designated reporting entities under Pakistan's AML Act 2010 and FATF recommendations.

**Customer Due Diligence (CDD):**
Before accepting any investor:
- Verify identity (passport, national ID)
- Verify source of funds ("how did you earn the money you are investing?")
- Beneficial ownership: identify ultimate beneficial owner (UBO) of any corporate investors
- PEP screening: check for Politically Exposed Persons
- Sanctions screening: check against UN, US OFAC, EU sanctions lists

**Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):**
Higher-risk investors require more verification:
- High-risk jurisdictions (FATF grey/black list countries)
- PEPs (politicians, government officials, their families/associates)
- Cash-intensive businesses (even if legal)

**Ongoing monitoring:**
- Annual refresh of CDD documentation
- Transaction monitoring: flag unusual patterns (large transfers, unusual trading activity, round-tripping)
- Suspicious Transaction Report (STR): file with Financial Monitoring Unit (FMU) if suspicious activity detected

**FERROQUANT AML program:**
- Dedicated MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer) — can be combined with CCO for small firms
- KYC completed before first investment by any investor
- Annual CDD refresh
- STR filing procedure clearly documented
- Staff AML training annually

### Regulatory Change Management

Regulations change constantly. FERROQUANT needs a process for:
1. **Horizon scanning:** Monitor SECP, SBP, DFSA, FATF updates. Use regulatory alert services (Thomson Reuters Regulatory Intelligence, Deloitte regulatory updates).
2. **Impact assessment:** When a new regulation is published, assess which aspects of operations are affected.
3. **Policy update:** Update the compliance manual within 30 days of a regulation taking effect.
4. **Training:** Ensure all relevant staff understand the new requirement.
5. **Documentation:** Maintain records of the regulatory change and the firm's response.
