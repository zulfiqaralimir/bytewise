# Module 131 — Breaking Into Investment Banking

Breaking into investment banking is one of the most competitive processes in the professional world. The process is highly structured in the US and UK (OCR recruiting from target universities), but in Pakistan and the Gulf, it is far more relationship-driven and less standardized. This module gives you the practical roadmap — from building the profile to surviving the superday.

## Learning Objectives

- Build an IB-ready CV, profile, and skills base
- Execute a targeted networking strategy for Pakistan and Gulf IB recruiting
- Prepare for technical and behavioral IB interviews
- Navigate the Pakistan and Gulf IB recruitment process (which is different from Western OCR)
- Position for lateral entry into IB from adjacent roles (audit, consulting, corporate finance)

---

## 1. The IB Recruiting Landscape

### Pakistan vs. Gulf vs. Western Recruiting

**Western OCR (On-Campus Recruiting):**
- Structured timeline: applications in September, superdays in November, offers by December
- Target universities: LSE, Oxbridge, Imperial, Warwick, LBS (UK); Princeton, Harvard, Wharton, Columbia (US)
- Structured internship pipeline: summer analysts → full-time analysts

**Gulf IB Recruiting:**
- Less structured; relationship-driven
- Most hires happen through referrals and direct approaches
- International banks in Dubai/Riyadh prefer candidates from global target schools OR strong local graduates with 2–4 years of relevant experience
- IB internships exist but less formalized than Western programs

**Pakistan IB Recruiting:**
- Almost entirely relationship-based; no OCR equivalent
- Major firms: Arif Habib, JS Global, Next Capital hire through referrals, business school connections, and direct outreach
- ICAP (Institute of Chartered Accountants Pakistan) qualification is a major credential — many Pakistan IB professionals are CAs
- International universities: LUMS (IBA), IBA Karachi, NUST are target schools for local IB firms

## 2. Building the IB Profile

### Essential Credentials

**Degree:**
- LUMS / IBA (Pakistan): Recognized by local firms; strong alumni network
- AGA Khan University / NUST: Emerging
- Abroad (LSE, Warwick, NUS, INSEAD, LBS): Opens international IB doors

**Professional qualifications:**
- **CA (ICAP):** Gold standard for Pakistan IB and corporate finance
- **CFA:** Widely respected; Level 3 passes signal commitment to finance
- **MBA (top-tier):** For post-experience entry (INSEAD, LBS, Wharton, Harvard for international; LUMS for Pakistan)

**Skills:**
- Excel modeling: build financial models from scratch (DCF, LBO, merger model)
- PowerPoint: IB-quality presentations (clean, data-driven, client-ready)
- Bloomberg/Refinitiv: terminal proficiency
- SECP/PSX mechanics: regulatory and market knowledge for Pakistan roles

### The Analyst CV

IB CVs follow a rigid format:
- One page only
- No objective statement; leads with education then experience
- Quantify everything: "Assisted on PKR 3.5B TFC issuance" not "worked on bond deals"
- Action verbs: advised, modeled, executed, analyzed, coordinated
- No fluff: every line must demonstrate financial/analytical competence

## 3. Networking Strategy

### Pakistan IB Networking

**LinkedIn:**
Connect with Arif Habib, JS Global, Next Capital analysts and associates. Request informational calls — frame it as learning, not asking for a job.

**ICAP events:**
ICAP hosts industry events attended by senior finance professionals including IB bankers.

**University alumni:**
LUMS, IBA, and LSE alumni in IB are the most accessible via alumni networks.

**Cold outreach approach:**
> "Dear [Name], I am a LUMS MBA student deeply interested in capital markets advisory. I have been following [firm's] work on [specific recent deal]. Could I request 20 minutes for a call to learn about your experience in IB? I will prepare specific questions."

**Key insight:** Pakistani IB professionals are generally willing to help — most didn't have formal support when they broke in. Personalize the outreach with knowledge of their firm's recent deals.

### Gulf IB Networking

**DIFC events:** DIFC hosts regular finance industry events — attend actively.

**Pakistani diaspora network:** Gulf has 1.5M+ Pakistani professionals, including many in finance. The Pakistani community in Dubai/Abu Dhabi is a significant networking resource.

**Referral culture:** In the Gulf, being referred by someone the hiring partner trusts is worth 10 cold applications.

## 4. Technical Interview Preparation

### Core Technical Areas

**Accounting and financial statements:**
- Walk me through the three financial statements
- How does an increase in accounts receivable affect cash flow?
- What is EBITDA and why is it used in valuation?

**Valuation:**
- Walk me through a DCF
- When would you use EV/EBITDA vs. P/E?
- When is trading comps not the right methodology?

**M&A:**
- Walk me through an accretion/dilution analysis
- What are synergies and how do you quantify them?
- Why might a company prefer an asset sale to a share sale?

**LBO:**
- Walk me through an LBO model
- What are the main drivers of LBO returns?
- What makes a good LBO candidate?

**Markets:**
- What happens to bond prices when interest rates rise?
- What is the current Pakistan benchmark rate? (Know this cold)
- What was the last major deal in the Pakistan market?

### Sample Answer: Walk Me Through a DCF

"A DCF values a company by projecting its future free cash flows and discounting them back to the present. I start by projecting FCFF for 5 years — revenue growth, EBITDA margins, capex, and working capital changes drive free cash flow. Then I calculate WACC — using CAPM for the cost of equity and the after-tax cost of debt, weighted by the target capital structure. Finally, I calculate terminal value using either the Gordon Growth Model or the exit multiple method. Summing the PV of explicit FCFs and the PV of terminal value gives enterprise value. Deducting net debt gives equity value."

## 5. The Interview Process

### Superday Format

**Typical Pakistan IB interview process:**
1. CV screening + phone screen (30 min with analyst/associate)
2. Technical test (financial modeling test; written case study)
3. Final round (2–3 interviews with VPs and managing directors)

**Gulf IB interview process:**
1. Application + recruiter screening
2. Technical interview (often remote — modeling test)
3. Superday (3–5 back-to-back interviews; mix of technical and behavioral)

### Behavioral Interview Questions

The most important behavioral questions for IB:

- "Tell me about yourself" (your 90-second story linking background → IB)
- "Why investment banking?" (must show genuine interest in advisory, not just the money)
- "Why this firm specifically?" (research recent deals; know the firm's culture)
- "Tell me about a time you worked on a deal/project under a tight deadline"
- "Walk me through your most complex financial analysis"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

## 6. Non-Target and Lateral Entry Strategies

### From Big 4 Audit

Big 4 audit is one of the best feeders into Pakistan IB:
- Financial statement expertise
- Client exposure
- ICAP qualification
- Path: audit → transaction advisory (TAS) / valuations at Big 4 → IB lateral hire

### From Corporate Finance / FP&A

Strong analytical profiles with DCF and modeling skills from corporate finance teams can lateral into IB:
- Build on existing modeling skills (add LBO and merger models)
- Network into IB through existing corporate finance client relationships

### From Consulting

Strategy consulting (McKinsey, BCG, OC&C) is a common source of IB laterals — especially for M&A advisory roles where strategic thinking is valued alongside financial modeling.
