# Module 99 — Precedent Transaction Analysis

Precedent transaction analysis ("deal comps" or "transaction comps") asks: what have acquirers actually paid for similar businesses in past M&A deals? Unlike trading comps — which reflect minority-stake public market pricing — transaction comps capture the price a willing buyer pays to acquire control of a business, including the control premium and any synergy value passed through to the seller. Transaction comps are the most powerful evidence of what a business is "really worth" in an M&A context.

## Learning Objectives

- Use deal databases (Mergermarket, Refinitiv, Capital IQ) to identify relevant precedent transactions
- Calculate transaction multiples (EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, P/BV) from deal announcements
- Understand the control premium and why transaction multiples exceed trading multiples
- Adjust for synergies in transaction multiple analysis
- Present a clean precedent transaction table for a pitch book
- Apply transaction comps to Gulf banking sector and Pakistan M&A deals

---

## 1. Precedent Transactions vs. Trading Comps

### Why Transaction Multiples Are Higher

**Control premium:** When acquiring 100% of a business, the buyer pays a premium above the public market price (the minority trading multiple). This premium — typically 20–40% for strategic acquirers — compensates the selling shareholders for:
- Giving up liquidity (they can no longer sell small amounts at market)
- Transferring control (the acquirer now has the ability to direct the business)
- The option value that shareholders had to hold and see the stock appreciate

**Synergy value passed through:** Strategic acquirers may be willing to pay more because they expect to extract synergies (cost savings, revenue uplift) that increase the combined entity's value. In a competitive auction, sellers capture some of this synergy value through price.

**Typical premium over trading comps:**
- Strategic acquirers: 20–40% control premium (transaction multiple vs. unaffected trading multiple)
- Financial acquirers (PE): Lower premium; disciplined by LBO return requirements

**Example:**
- Cement company trading at 5.0x EBITDA (public market)
- Acquired by a strategic buyer at 7.0x EBITDA (transaction)
- Implied control premium: (7.0 − 5.0) / 5.0 = 40%

## 2. Deal Databases and Data Sources

### Professional Deal Databases

**Mergermarket (ION Analytics):**
Covers announced and completed M&A globally. Strong coverage of sub-$1B deals and emerging markets. Subscription: $20,000–50,000/year.

**Refinitiv (LSEG) / Eikon:**
Comprehensive deal database integrated with financial data. Standard at most bulge brackets and large regional banks.

**Capital IQ (S&P Global):**
Widely used for financial data and transaction screening. Strong on company financials; good transaction coverage for US and Europe. Standard at most IB firms.

**Bloomberg M&A:**
Less comprehensive than dedicated deal databases but free for Bloomberg terminal subscribers.

**Free/lower-cost sources:**
- SEC EDGAR (EDGAR has 8-K filings for US deals with merger consideration and deal terms)
- PSX company announcements (material information disclosures for Pakistani listed company transactions)
- SECP filings (Pakistan merger control filings)
- Press releases and news (for announced but not yet closed deals)

### Searching for Precedent Transactions

**Screening criteria for transaction search:**
1. Target industry: cement / banking / FMCG / telecom (match the target's industry)
2. Geography: Pakistan / South Asia / MENA / comparable EM market
3. Deal type: acquisition of majority stake (not minority investments or JVs — different dynamics)
4. Time period: Last 5–7 years (older transactions may be less relevant due to market changes)
5. Deal size: ±1 order of magnitude of the target deal size

**Starting broad, then narrowing:**
Begin with a broad screen (e.g., all MENA banking M&A in the last 7 years), then narrow by removing outliers (distressed transactions, government-directed deals, tiny tuck-ins).

## 3. Calculating Transaction Multiples

### The Data Challenge

Unlike trading comps (where financial data is in quarterly/annual reports), transaction comps require finding the target company's financials at the time of the deal — which may be:
- Disclosed in the deal press release or announcement
- In analyst research from the deal period
- In SEC filings (for US deals) or equivalent regulatory filings
- In the information memorandum (if you worked on the deal)
- Imputed from public data (reverse-engineer from partial disclosures)

**Missing data:** For smaller deals and private company acquisitions, EBITDA at time of deal is often not publicly disclosed. In this case:
- Use what is disclosed (typically revenue; sometimes EBITDA margin can be estimated)
- Note "NM" (Not Meaningful) in the comps table for unavailable metrics
- Use analyst estimates from the deal period

### Calculating Enterprise Value from Deal Terms

**Cash deal:**
```
Enterprise Value = Transaction Value (Total consideration paid)
                 + Target's net debt at closing
```

Or equivalently:
```
Enterprise Value = Equity purchase price + Assumed debt − Assumed cash
```

**Stock deal:**
The equity consideration is the number of shares issued to target shareholders × acquirer's share price at announcement.

**Earn-out:**
If deal includes future contingent payments (earn-outs), these are typically excluded from the headline transaction multiple (since they're conditional). Disclose earn-out separately.

### LTM Metrics at Time of Deal

Always use target's **LTM financials at deal announcement date** — not the most recent available data. A deal announced in October 2022 should use the target's LTM EBITDA ending September 2022, not the full-year 2022 EBITDA (which wasn't available at announcement).

**Annualizing partial year:**
If only YTD data is available, annualize carefully:
- For steady-state businesses: (YTD / months elapsed) × 12
- For seasonal businesses: use prior year full-year as a proxy, then adjust for YTD trend

## 4. Worked Example: Gulf Banking Sector Transaction Comps

### Selected Gulf Banking M&A Transactions

| Year | Target | Acquirer | Country | Deal Value ($B) | P/BV | P/E (LTM) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2020 | Samba Financial Group | National Commercial Bank | Saudi Arabia | 14.8 | 1.3x | 14.2x |
| 2019 | Union National Bank | Abu Dhabi Commercial Bank | UAE | 2.5 | 1.1x | 11.5x |
| 2017 | NBAD + FGB merger | First Abu Dhabi Bank | UAE | 29.0 | 1.8x | 16.0x |
| 2019 | Bahrain Islamic Bank | Khaleeji Commercial Bank | Bahrain | 0.6 | 0.9x | 8.2x |
| 2021 | Qatar Islamic Bank / Masraf | QIB consortium | Qatar | 1.4 | 1.4x | 11.8x |
| 2022 | Bank Muscat minority stake | Raiffeisen Intl exit | Oman | 0.4 | 0.8x | 7.5x |

**Summary statistics:**

| Metric | Min | 25th % | Median | 75th % | Max |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| P/BV | 0.8x | 0.9x | 1.25x | 1.45x | 1.8x |
| P/E (LTM) | 7.5x | 8.9x | 11.7x | 14.5x | 16.0x |

**Key observations:**
- Larger, higher-ROE banks command P/BV premiums (NBAD/FGB at 1.8x for a "merger of equals" among top-tier UAE banks)
- Distressed or sub-scale banks trade at or below book (0.8–0.9x P/BV)
- Islamic bank transactions (Bahrain Islamic, QIB) trade at similar multiples to conventional banks — Shariah compliance is no longer a valuation discount

### Applying to a Pakistani Bank Acquisition

**Hypothetical target:** A mid-tier Pakistani commercial bank
- Book value: PKR 45B
- LTM net income: PKR 6B
- ROE: 13.3%

**Applying Gulf transaction comps (adjusted for Pakistan discount):**
- Gulf banks command higher multiples than Pakistan banks (Gulf banks have stronger balance sheets, lower NPLs, stronger regulatory frameworks)
- Pakistan discount: approximately 20–30% to Gulf comps

```
Gulf P/BV median: 1.25x → Pakistan-adjusted: ~0.9–1.1x
Gulf P/E median: 11.7x → Pakistan-adjusted: ~8–10x

Implied equity value:
At 1.0x P/BV: PKR 45B
At 9x P/E: PKR 54B
Range: PKR 45–54B
```

## 5. Control Premium Analysis

### Calculating the Premium to Unaffected Share Price

When a public company is acquired, the control premium is measured relative to the **unaffected share price** — the price before any deal speculation was reflected:

```
Control Premium = (Offer Price − Unaffected Share Price) / Unaffected Share Price
```

**Unaffected price:** Typically the share price 1 trading day before the announcement, or the 30-day VWAP before rumors emerged (whichever is earlier and cleaner).

**Why unaffected price matters:** If a leak occurred, the market price before announcement already reflects partial deal speculation. Using that price understates the true premium paid.

### Control Premium Database

Historical control premiums for comparable transactions give acquirers a market reference:

**Typical control premium ranges:**
| Deal Type | Typical Premium |
|---|---|
| Strategic acquirer, friendly deal | 25–40% |
| Strategic acquirer, hostile takeover | 40–60% |
| Financial acquirer (PE) | 15–30% |
| Distressed acquisition | 0–15% |
| Government/privatization | 5–20% |

**Pakistan specific:** Pakistan has limited hostile takeover precedent (SECP Takeover Regulations 2017 require mandatory offer at acquisition of 25%+). Most acquisitions are friendly. Historical control premiums on PSX have ranged 15–35%.

## 6. Synergy Adjustment in Transaction Multiples

### Why Synergies Affect the Multiple

A strategic acquirer may pay 8x EBITDA for a target whose standalone value is 6x EBITDA. The 2x premium reflects synergy value passed through to the seller. Including this synergy-inflated transaction multiple in comps without adjustment overstates the pure "standalone" value of the business.

**Synergy-adjusted multiple:**
```
Synergy-Adjusted Multiple = Transaction Multiple − (PV of Synergies / LTM EBITDA)
```

**Example:**
- Transaction multiple: 8.0x EBITDA
- Disclosed synergies: $50M annual cost savings, worth $350M PV (7x)
- Target LTM EBITDA: $100M
- Synergy contribution per EBITDA multiple: $350M / $100M = 3.5x
- Standalone value implied: 8.0x − 3.5x × (share passed to seller estimate ~50%) = ~6.25x

**In practice:** Full synergy disclosure is rare. Use judgment — if an acquirer explicitly states large synergies, flag the transaction multiple as "synergy-elevated" in the comps table.
