# Module 125 — FIG Advisory In Depth

Financial Institutions Group (FIG) advisory is the most technically specialized area of investment banking. Banks, insurance companies, and asset managers have fundamentally different financial structures from industrial companies — and advising on their M&A and capital raising requires mastering P&L attribution, regulatory capital, embedded value, and banking-specific valuation frameworks. This module goes deep into FIG advisory across banking, insurance, and FinTech.

## Learning Objectives

- Value a bank for M&A (P/BV, P/TBV, excess capital frameworks)
- Structure a bank merger advisory mandate
- Advise on insurance company M&A (embedded value, combined ratio)
- Execute FinTech advisory and understand digital banking M&A dynamics
- Navigate SBP-directed bank mergers in Pakistan
- Analyze Gulf FIG mega-transactions (FAB creation, Emirates NBD-DenizBank)

## 1. Bank M&A — Valuation and Process

### Why Bank Valuation Is Different

Banks cannot be valued using EV/EBITDA because:
- Interest income is a revenue item (not subtracted like interest expense in industrial companies)
- "EBITDA" for a bank is meaningless — the "inventory" is loans; "cost of goods sold" is the cost of deposits
- Balance sheet quality (NPL ratio, capital adequacy) drives value as much as earnings

**Bank valuation metrics:**
- **P/BV (Price-to-Book Value):** Market cap / book equity. Banks with ROE > cost of equity trade at P/BV > 1.0x; below-cost-of-equity ROE → P/BV < 1.0x
- **P/TBV (Price-to-Tangible Book Value):** Excludes goodwill and intangibles — cleaner for acquisition accounting
- **P/E:** Standard earnings multiple
- **Gordon Growth Model for banks:** P/BV = (ROE − g) / (Ke − g) — links valuation directly to returns and growth

**Key bank profitability metrics:**
- ROE (Return on Equity): Net income / equity
- NIM (Net Interest Margin): Net interest income / average earning assets
- Cost-to-Income (CIR): Operating costs / operating income
- NPL ratio: Non-performing loans / total loans

### Bank M&A Process Considerations

**Capital adequacy at closing:**
The acquirer must ensure the combined entity meets regulatory capital requirements (SBP minimum 12.5% CAR for Pakistan banks; Basel III CET1 for international). If the target has a capital deficit, the acquirer must inject capital.

**Portfolio quality:**
- NPL due diligence: independent review of loan portfolio; identify loans likely to deteriorate post-acquisition
- Provision adequacy: are existing provisions sufficient? Underprovided NPLs are effectively hidden liabilities

**Branch network:**
For retail banks, the branch network is a key value driver. Post-merger, overlapping branches are rationalized (cost synergy).

**IT systems:**
Core banking system (CBS) integration is the most technically complex and expensive integration workstream. Most Pakistan banks run on Temenos, Oracle FLEXCUBE, or Finacle.

## 2. SBP-Directed Bank Mergers in Pakistan

### The SBP Consolidation Agenda

SBP has periodically pushed for banking sector consolidation to:
- Improve capital adequacy ratios of sub-scale banks
- Reduce the number of banks to improve systemic stability
- Create larger, more competitive banks capable of financing large projects

**Recent SBP-directed or encouraged mergers:**
- Burj Bank → Allied Bank (2017): Islamic window acquired
- Silk Bank: Merger discussions with FINCA
- First Women Bank: Merger discussions with other government banks
- Summit Bank: Capital concerns; merger with Sindh Bank considered

**SBP process for bank mergers:**
Under the Banking Companies Ordinance 1962:
1. Banks submit merger proposal to SBP
2. SBP reviews (capital adequacy, management quality, customer interest)
3. Court-sanctioned scheme of arrangement
4. Shareholders/depositors rights protected
5. Employees: SECP and SBP guidelines on post-merger redundancy

## 3. Insurance M&A

### Insurance Valuation: Embedded Value

Insurance company valuation requires "embedded value" — the intrinsic value of the existing in-force policy portfolio:

```
Embedded Value = Net Asset Value (capital + surplus) + Value of In-Force Business (VIF)
```

**VIF:** PV of future profits from existing policies, net of capital cost.

**Key insurance M&A metrics:**
- P/EV (Price-to-Embedded Value)
- Combined ratio: (Claims + Expenses) / Premiums. Below 100% = underwriting profit
- Return on equity

**Pakistan insurance market:**
State Life Insurance (government), Jubilee Life, EFU Life are the major life insurers. Jubilee General Insurance is the largest listed general insurer. Market is underpenetrated (premium/GDP ~1% vs. 3-5% for developed markets).

## 4. Gulf FIG Case Studies

### First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB) — 2017

**Transaction:** National Bank of Abu Dhabi (NBAD) + First Gulf Bank (FGB) merger

**Rationale:**
- Create the UAE's largest and the Arab world's largest bank by assets
- Combined balance sheet of $182B
- UAE government-backed (NBAD was the largest government bank)

**Structure:** All-share exchange. FGB shareholders received 1.254 NBAD shares per FGB share.

**Exchange ratio determination:**
Relative valuations of both banks were analyzed; exchange ratio reflected FGB's higher profitability (ROE) vs. NBAD's larger size.

**Outcome:** FAB has grown to $330B+ assets; consistently ranks among the world's safest banks.

### Emirates NBD — DenizBank Acquisition (2019)

**Transaction:** Emirates NBD acquired DenizBank (Turkey's 6th largest bank) from Sberbank (Russia) for $3.2B.

**Strategic rationale:**
- Gain exposure to Turkey's large economy and growing banking sector
- Diversify beyond UAE home market
- Access to Turkish diaspora banking and trade finance flows

**Complexity:**
- Cross-border bank acquisition (UAE regulator + Turkish BDDK approval)
- Turkish lira depreciation risk: TRY depreciated significantly between signing and closing
- Geopolitical risk: Russia/Sberbank as vendor; UAE-Turkey relations
- Required significant capital injection post-close as TRY depreciation eroded capital ratios

## Self-Assessment

1. You are advising NBAD on the merger with First Gulf Bank (recreating the FAB deal). Both banks are PSX equivalents in Pakistan:
   - Bank A: PKR 600B assets, 14% ROE, 1.1x P/BV
   - Bank B: PKR 450B assets, 18% ROE, 1.4x P/BV

   (a) Calculate fair exchange ratio using relative P/BV values. (b) Bank B shareholders argue they should receive a premium because they have higher ROE. Bank A shareholders argue the premium is excessive. How do you resolve this in the exchange ratio negotiation? (c) SBP requires the combined entity to maintain 12.5% CAR. Bank A has CAR 14%; Bank B has CAR 13%. What is the combined CAR before any synergies or adjustments? (d) Post-merger, 80 overlapping branches will be closed. Calculate the NPV of this synergy: PKR 8M annual rent savings per branch + PKR 2M overhead, at 15% discount rate, assuming closures happen over 3 years.

2. A Gulf insurance company is acquiring a Pakistani insurance company. Target: PKR 15B EV, 85% combined ratio, growing at 20%/year. (a) Calculate P/EV at PKR 18B offer. (b) The target's combined ratio has been 85–95% for the last 5 years, varying significantly with major weather events (floods). How should this variability affect the valuation? (c) SBP's Insurance Ordinance 2000 requires foreign ownership approval. What conditions might SBP attach to this acquisition approval?
