# Module 92 — Quant Finance in Pakistan & Gulf

This module is FERROQUANT Capital's full story — from idea to operational fund. It synthesizes everything in Track B: probability theory, stochastic calculus, options pricing, fixed income mathematics, trading systems, portfolio construction, hedge fund strategies, risk management, regulation, legal structures, tax, ESG, and Islamic finance. Pakistan and Gulf are not afterthoughts — they are the primary market where FERROQUANT operates and where the unique alpha sources live.

## Learning Objectives

- Identify the structural alpha opportunities unique to Pakistan's capital markets
- Map the Gulf's capital and investor landscape for an emerging quant fund
- Design FERROQUANT's regulatory path from inception to full operational status
- Build FERROQUANT's data infrastructure using available Pakistan/Gulf data
- Design a realistic capital raising strategy targeting Pakistani and GCC investors

## 1. Pakistan Capital Market: Structural Characteristics

### PSX Market Microstructure

**Market size:** KSE-100 Index market cap ~$40–60B (2024), total PSX listed market cap ~$80–100B. Small relative to India ($3T), but larger than most central Asian and African markets.

**Trading characteristics:**
- Average daily turnover: PKR 15–25B (~USD 60–90M)
- Top 5 stocks (by turnover): HBL, OGDCL, MCB, Lucky, Engro — these 5 stocks often represent 30–40% of daily turnover
- Price limits (circuit breakers): ±7.5% intraday price movement limit on individual stocks
- Settlement: T+2 (Ready Market) and T+0 (Deliveries Market for large block trades)
- Short selling: Limited. PSX has a formal securities lending framework, but liquidity in the securities lending market is very thin

**Institutional investor landscape:**
- Mutual fund industry: ~PKR 2.5T AUM (IDBs, conventional funds, pension funds)
- Insurance companies: PKR 1.5T+ (State Life, Jubilee, EFU)
- Pension funds: EOBI, SESSI (government pension funds), private sector PESA funds
- Banks' proprietary trading desks: Major players (HBL, NBP, UBL, SCB)
- Foreign portfolio investors (FPIs): Minimal — PSX has seen FPI outflows post-2018 political uncertainty

### PSX Alpha Sources: What Works

**1. Momentum (12-1 month):**
Historical IC on PSX: 0.06–0.08 (moderate-to-strong for an EM market).

**Why momentum works in Pakistan:**
- Retail investor dominance (60–70% of daily trading is retail): retail investors underreact initially, then chase trends
- Information diffusion is slow: earnings surprises are gradually incorporated into prices over weeks
- Limited short selling: negative information can't be incorporated quickly, prolonging upside momentum

**2. Value (Price-to-Book, P/E):**
Historical IC on PSX: 0.04–0.06.

**Why value works:**
- PSX has cyclically cheap sectors (energy, banking) that periodically re-rate on macro catalyst
- Value-growth spread is large (P/E 5–8x for value vs. 15–25x for growth) — more room for mean reversion than developed markets

**3. Governance Quality (G-Factor):**
FERROQUANT's proprietary signal. IC estimated 0.07–0.09 based on backtests.

**Why governance works:**
- Pakistan's corporate governance is heterogeneous — quality varies dramatically
- Governance improvements (hiring Big 4 auditors, adding independent directors, reducing related-party transactions) are predictive of positive price moves
- Governance deterioration (auditor resignation, director resignation, material weakness disclosures) predicts sharp downward moves

**4. Interest Rate Macro (PIB Cycle):**
When SBP cuts rates, the entire financial sector re-rates (banks' NIM improves, PIBs gain in value, equity risk premium compresses). Anticipating the rate-cut cycle is one of Pakistan's most powerful macro trades.

**5. Earnings Surprise:**
PSX companies report quarterly earnings (unlike many frontier markets which report semi-annually). Earnings surprise signal has IC of 0.05–0.08 in the first month after announcement.

**6. FX/Macro Regime Signal:**
PKR stability → risk-on for PSX → positive equity factor
PKR devaluation → risk-off → negative equity factor + rates spike
Monitoring IMF program milestones as a leading indicator of PKR stability.

### PSX Alpha Sources: What Doesn't Work

- **High-frequency trading:** No HFT ecosystem, market is not fast enough to reward sub-second strategies
- **Pure factor investing with no macro overlay:** Pakistan's macro regime risk swamps pure factor signals in crisis periods (2022–2023)
- **Statistical arbitrage on PSX pairs:** Too thin liquidity for pairs trading; bid-ask spreads are too wide to profit from small price discrepancies
- **Short selling strategies:** No liquid securities lending market

## 2. Gulf Capital Market: Investment Landscape

### GCC Equity Markets

**Saudi Exchange (Tadawul):**
- Largest GCC market: $2T+ market cap
- Highly liquid for large-caps (Saudi Aramco, Al Rajhi Bank, Saudi National Bank)
- MSCI Emerging Markets inclusion since 2019 → significant foreign institutional flows
- Momentum and value factors both documented in Tadawul research
- FERROQUANT access via Qualified Foreign Investor (QFI) registration

**Dubai Financial Market (DFM) and Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange (ADX):**
- Combined cap ~$700B
- Key sectors: real estate (Emaar), banking (FAB, Emirates NBD), telecom (du, Etisalat)
- Less liquid than Tadawul; sector concentration risk
- FERROQUANT access: direct as a DIFC-registered manager

**Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain:**
- Kuwait Stock Exchange and Qatar Stock Exchange: significant oil wealth markets
- MSCI EM inclusion for both
- Less liquid than Saudi/UAE; factor strategies face higher transaction cost hurdle

### GCC Fixed Income

**Gulf sovereign sukuk / bonds:**
- UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia all issue USD-denominated sovereign sukuk
- Investment-grade (AA/A rated) at small spreads over US Treasuries
- Key institutional buyers: ADIA, QIA, Islamic banks
- FERROQUANT opportunity: sovereign sukuk as carry + duration portfolio for Islamic mandate

**GCC investment-grade corporate bonds:**
- Aramco, ADNOC, Emirates NBD corporate bonds
- High credit quality, liquid secondary market
- USD-denominated → no EM currency risk for FERROQUANT's USD-mandate accounts

### Gulf LP Investor Landscape

**Tier 1 — Sovereign Wealth Funds ($100B+ AUM each):**
- ADIA (Abu Dhabi): $993B, diversified global allocation including EM equities
- QIA (Qatar): $475B, global allocation
- PIF (Saudi Arabia Public Investment Fund): $700B+, increasingly active in EM
- Mubadala (Abu Dhabi): $276B, technology and energy focused

**Minimum requirements to access SWF capital:**
- 3-year track record with meaningful AUM (minimum $100–300M to be considered)
- Risk-adjusted returns demonstrating alpha over benchmark
- SECP or international regulatory registration
- Robust compliance, risk management, and reporting infrastructure
- ESG integration (see Module 90)
- On-site due diligence visits (portfolio managers must travel to Abu Dhabi / Riyadh)

**Tier 2 — GCC Family Offices ($500M–$5B AUM):**
- Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Riyadh, Kuwait City family offices
- More flexible than SWFs: can invest at smaller AUM ($5–50M ticket sizes)
- Relationship-driven: requires introduction through network (law firms, prime brokers, DIFC/ADGM networking events)
- Islamic finance preference: many GCC family offices prefer Shariah-compliant structures

**Tier 3 — Pakistan HNWI and UHNWIs:**
- Growing HNWI population: ~150,000 Pakistani HNWIs (estimated, $1M+ investable assets)
- Diaspora: Gulf-based NRPs are a key source — proven appetite for Pakistan investment opportunities
- Access: through private banking relationships (HBL Private, MCB Gold, Arif Habib Elite), wealth management conferences, and direct referrals

## 3. FERROQUANT's Regulatory Path

### Phase 1: Pakistan AMC / NBFC License (Months 1–12)

**Step 1 — Incorporate the management company:**
- Private Limited Company under Companies Act 2017
- Registered office in Karachi (SECP jurisdiction)
- Minimum paid-up capital: PKR 200M for AMC license; PKR 30M for NBFC-IM (lower bar, less scope)

**Step 2 — Apply for NBFC-IM license (Investment Management) from SECP:**
- Application: Form I (individual fit & proper), Form II (company fit & proper)
- Key documents: business plan, compliance manual, risk management policy, organogram
- Processing time: 3–6 months
- Requirements: minimum 2 qualified portfolio managers (CFA/ACCA/MBA finance)

**Step 3 — Launch first product (Pakistan AIF):**
- Apply for AIF registration (Alternative Investment Fund) — minimum PKR 10M per investor
- Trustee appointment (scheduled bank — HBL, UBL, or MCB as trustee)
- Offering document filing with SECP

### Phase 2: DIFC QIF Registration (Months 6–18)

**Step 1 — Incorporate DIFC management company:**
- DIFC Company (LLC or branch) — minimum capital AED 500,000 (~$136K)
- Apply for DFSA Category 3C license (managing collective investment funds)
- Processing time: 4–6 months (DFSA is thorough but efficient)
- Requirements: Fit & proper individuals, compliance officer, risk management function, office in DIFC

**Step 2 — Register the QIF:**
- File QIF application with DFSA
- Appoint a DFSA-registered administrator and auditor
- Set up Cayman master fund (legal counsel: Cayman law firm)

**Step 3 — Open prime brokerage and custodian accounts:**
- Pakistan: Arif Habib Limited, Next Capital, or JS Global as local broker/custodian
- International: Interactive Brokers, Goldman Sachs, or Morgan Stanley prime (for international access)

## 5. Capital Raising Strategy

### The Seeding Problem

New quant funds face the "chicken and egg" problem:
- Institutional investors require a 3-year track record before investing
- Without institutional capital, it's impossible to build a track record quickly
- The only escape: **seed capital from insiders, family, friends, or a seeder fund**

**FERROQUANT seeding options:**

**Option A: Proprietary capital seed**
- Founders and family contribute PKR 50–200M (USD 200–700K) as initial seed
- Build track record over 2–3 years using founder capital
- Risk: founders bear 100% of downside during track record building period
- Advantage: full strategy control, no external pressure

**Option B: Pakistan HNWI seed**
- Raise PKR 200–500M from 5–10 Pakistani HNWI investors (PKR 20–50M per investor)
- Access through personal network, private banking relationships, NRP diaspora
- Requirements: SECP-compliant AIF, Offering Document, Subscription Agreements
- Timeline: 6–12 months to close initial investors

**Option C: Gulf family office seed**
- A DIFC/Dubai-based family office provides $5–20M as seed capital
- In exchange: discounted management fee (1% vs. standard 1.5%) for first 2 years
- Often involves seeder co-investment in the management company (equity stake)
- Access: through law firms (Clifford Chance DIFC, Al Tamimi), prime brokers, DIFC networking

### Institutional Capital Raising (After 2–3 Year Track Record)

**Phase 2 capital target: $50–200M**

**Roadshow preparation:**
1. **Pitch book:** Strategy overview (1-2 pages), risk framework, returns attribution, team bios, legal structure, terms
2. **Tear sheet:** 1-page factsheet — key statistics (returns, Sharpe, drawdown, AUM)
3. **Due diligence questionnaire (DDQ):** 100+ question standard questionnaire covering every aspect of fund operations — often the Institutional Limited Partners Association (ILPA) DDQ
4. **References:** 2–3 existing investors willing to speak to prospective investors

**Target institutions (priority order for FERROQUANT):**
1. Gulf family offices (DIFC/ADGM) — $5–25M tickets, fastest decision process (6–12 months)
2. Pakistan institutional investors (insurance companies, pension funds) — PKR 500M–5B tickets, SECP compliance required
3. Gulf SWFs (ADIA, QIA, Mubadala) — $50M+ tickets, 12–24 month due diligence, 3-year track record required
4. European family offices through DIFC introduction — $2–10M tickets, SFDR Article 8 requirement

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### FERROQUANT's Competitive Advantage

**In Pakistan:**
1. **First-mover in systematic quant:** Pakistan has virtually no pure quantitative funds. FERROQUANT occupies a niche with no direct domestic competitors.
2. **Pakistan market expertise:** Quant strategies must be adapted for PSX microstructure, liquidity constraints, and macro regime dynamics. An international quant fund cannot simply apply its global model to Pakistan.
3. **Access advantage:** Pakistan data (SECP filings, SBP data, PSX statistics) is free and rich for a domestic player — an international fund would have to build this infrastructure from scratch.
4. **Regulatory relationships:** Early mover in SECP AIF framework → first-mover relationships with regulator.

**In the Gulf:**
1. **Pakistan-Gulf corridor play:** FERROQUANT connects Gulf capital to Pakistan's growth story — a unique investment access product for GCC investors who want Pakistan exposure without operational complexity.
2. **DIFC platform:** DIFC-regulated fund is credible to GCC institutional investors in ways that a Pakistan-only structure is not.
3. **Islamic finance capability:** Offering a Shariah-compliant version from inception captures Gulf Islamic capital that most international quant funds cannot.

### Competitive Risks

**Risk 1 — Regulatory risk:** SECP regulatory changes (stricter leverage rules, investment restrictions) can constrain strategy.

**Risk 2 — Liquidity risk:** PSX liquidity is thin. At $50M+ AUM, FERROQUANT's own trading starts to move the market, degrading signal quality.

**Risk 3 — Key-man risk:** If the lead portfolio manager leaves, the fund's track record loses continuity — a serious concern for institutional investors.

**Risk 4 — Currency risk for Gulf investors:** Gulf investors in the Pakistan AIF are exposed to PKR devaluation. FERROQUANT should offer PKR-hedged share classes where economically feasible.

**Risk 5 — International competition:** As Pakistan grows, large international EM funds (Lazard, GQG, Ashmore) may build Pakistan desks, increasing competition for the same alpha sources.
