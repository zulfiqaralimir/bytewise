# Module 113 — ECM in Gulf & Pakistan Markets

The Gulf experienced one of the most significant IPO waves in its history between 2021 and 2023, driven by Saudi Vision 2030 privatizations, ADNOC's subsidiary listing strategy, and buoyant oil-supported markets. Pakistan's ECM market operates differently — smaller, episodic, and dominated by domestic retail and institutional investors. Understanding both markets is essential for IB professionals targeting the region.

## Learning Objectives

- Analyze the Gulf ECM market structure and the 2021–2023 IPO wave
- Understand Saudi Aramco IPO mechanics and the Tadawul listing process
- Explain ADNOC's carve-out IPO strategy and its rationale
- Assess PSX IPO market characteristics and key institutional investors
- Understand Islamic ECM structures (Shariah-compliant IPOs)

---

## 1. Gulf ECM Market

### The 2021–2023 IPO Wave

The Gulf saw unprecedented ECM activity:

**Saudi Arabia (Tadawul):**
- Saudi Aramco (2019): $29.4B IPO — largest in history. Priced at SAR 32, valuing Aramco at $1.7 trillion.
- ACWA Power (2021): $1.2B IPO — largest Saudi non-energy IPO
- Saudi Telecom Company (stc) subsidiary listings
- NEOM-related companies beginning to IPO

**UAE (DFM/ADX):**
- ADNOC Drilling (2021): $1.1B IPO on ADX
- ADNOC Gas (2023): $2.5B IPO — largest UAE IPO in over a decade
- ADNOC Logistics & Services (2023): $769M IPO
- Empower (Dubai heating/cooling utility): $724M IPO (2022)
- DEWA (Dubai Electricity and Water Authority): $6.1B IPO (2022) — largest UAE IPO

**What drove the wave:**
- Vision 2030 mandates to reduce government ownership in strategic sectors
- High oil prices (2022) boosting valuations and investor confidence
- Gulf exchanges upgrading technology and regulation to attract institutional investors
- MSCI inclusion of Saudi Arabia (2019) and UAE bringing global passive flows

### ADNOC's Carve-Out Strategy

ADNOC (Abu Dhabi National Oil Company) has systematically listed subsidiaries while retaining majority ownership:
- Lists 20–30% free float in each subsidiary
- Raises capital for each business unit separately
- Establishes market values for each subsidiary (enabling benchmark for future transactions)
- Creates employee share plans tied to each listed subsidiary
- Abu Dhabi investor base benefits from access to world-class energy assets

**Banker's lesson:** ADNOC's strategy is a textbook SOTP unlock — carve-out IPOs to reduce holding company discount over time.

## 2. PSX ECM Market

### Market Characteristics

PSX IPO market features:
- **Retail-dominated:** Retail investors represent 60–70% of PSX daily volume; institutional less dominant than mature markets
- **Episodic:** IPO windows open and close with market sentiment; 2021 was a strong year; 2022–2023 very quiet due to macro crisis
- **Domestic investors only:** Most PSX IPOs are subscribed entirely by domestic investors; international investor participation is rare for all but the largest companies
- **Mutual fund constraints:** Pakistan's mutual fund industry is relatively small (PKR 2–3 trillion AUM) limiting institutional demand

**PSX IPO subscription mechanics:**
- Applications submitted via CDC or commercial bank branches
- Oversubscribed IPOs are balloted; each application has equal probability regardless of size (democratic allocation)
- This structure strongly favors retail participation — every applicant has the same probability of getting shares

### Islamic ECM

Shariah-compliant IPOs require:
- The company's business must pass Shariah screening (no prohibited activities, acceptable financial ratios)
- A Shariah Supervisory Board (SSB) certifies the offering
- Sukuk structures replace conventional bonds in the capital structure
- Profit-sharing certificates instead of interest-bearing instruments

**Gulf Islamic ECM:**
All major Tadawul IPOs require Shariah compliance review (given Saudi regulatory requirements). UAE companies listing on DFM have the option of conventional or Islamic structuring.
