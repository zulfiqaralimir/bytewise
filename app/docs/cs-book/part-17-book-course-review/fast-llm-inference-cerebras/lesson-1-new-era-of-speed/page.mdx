# Lesson 1: <span style={{ color: "#a5b4fc", fontWeight: 700 }}>The New Era of Inference Speed</span>

> *"As models converge on similar intelligence, the race shifts to who can deliver that intelligence fastest."*

---

## Phase 1: The Race for Intelligence

For years, AI labs competed purely on making models smarter.

This was driven by <span style={{ color: "#f43f5e", fontWeight: 700 }}>scaling laws</span> — a discovered pattern where increasing compute, data, and model size reliably reduced error and improved capability.

- This made progress **predictable**: scale up, and performance improves.
- At the same time, <span style={{ color: "#0ea5e9", fontWeight: 700 }}>context windows</span> (how much text a model can process at once) grew from thousands of tokens to millions.
- As benchmark scores climbed industry-wide, the intelligence gap between closed-source and open-source models narrowed significantly — <span style={{ color: "#84cc16", fontWeight: 700 }}>intelligence began to converge across the industry.</span>

---

## Phase 2: The Race for Speed

As models become similarly capable, intelligence alone stops being a differentiator.

> **A new race has begun: delivering that intelligence faster.**

### Three Key Latency Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|---|---|
| **Time to First Token (TTFT)** | How long before the model begins responding at all |
| **Tokens per Second** | How quickly the model generates output once it starts |
| **Inter-Token Latency (ITL)** | The delay between each generated token — lower means smoother, more natural streaming |

### Industry Proof Points

- <span style={{ color: "#fbbf24", fontWeight: 700 }}>Google's Gemini 3.1 Flash</span> — built for agentic coding, ~3x faster than Gemini 3 Pro
- <span style={{ color: "#d946ef", fontWeight: 700 }}>Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.8 fast mode</span> — same intelligence, up to 2.5x faster
- <span style={{ color: "#fb923c", fontWeight: 700 }}>OpenAI's Cerebras-powered GPT-5.3-Codex-Spark</span> — 1,000+ tokens/sec, OpenAI's fastest model to date

---

## Why Speed Matters More Now

- Capable models reason in multiple steps before answering.
- Agentic and multi-agent workflows chain dozens of model and tool calls together.
- <span style={{ color: "#22d3ee", fontWeight: 700 }}>Latency compounds across the workflow</span> even if any single call seems fast.

> **Analogy from the lesson:** like cellular networks moving from 1G to 5G, faster speed doesn't just make old things faster — it enables entirely new categories of application (video streaming, ride-sharing) that weren't previously practical. The same shift is expected in AI: voice agents, live translation, and real-time multi-agent coordination all depend on speed, not just intelligence.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The industry spent years racing for **intelligence**, driven by predictable scaling laws.
- As intelligence converges across labs, **speed** becomes the new differentiator.
- Three metrics define speed: **TTFT**, **tokens per second**, and **ITL**.
- Latency **compounds** across multi-step, agentic workflows — a fast single call doesn't guarantee a fast overall experience.
- History suggests speed doesn't just accelerate existing use cases — it unlocks entirely new ones.

> **Simple takeaway:** once models are smart enough, being fast is what turns "impressive demo" into "usable product."
