# Marketing Engineering

Marketing is not guesswork. It is a system.

Most engineers treat marketing as "someone else's job" — a mysterious blend of slogans, vibes, and luck. That's a mistake. The best companies in the world — Airbnb, Stripe, Notion, Linear — treat marketing the same way they treat software: as an engineering discipline. You build a model, test hypotheses, measure outcomes, and iterate.

**Marketing Engineering** is the application of systematic, analytical, and scalable thinking to how ideas, products, and brands reach people.

## The Engineering Mindset Applied to Marketing

Great marketing shares the same properties as great software:

- **It is designed** — not accidental. Every touchpoint is intentional.
- **It is measurable** — every campaign has a hypothesis and a metric.
- **It compounds** — good content, brand equity, and SEO build over time like a codebase.
- **It scales** — systems replace manual effort as you grow.

> **Key insight:** A bad product with great marketing will fail eventually. A great product with no marketing will also fail. The goal is to build both — and this part teaches you the marketing half.
