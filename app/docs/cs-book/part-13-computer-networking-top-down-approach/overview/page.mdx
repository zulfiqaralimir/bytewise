# Part 13 — Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach

**Based on:** *Computer Networking: A Top-Down Approach*, 8th Edition
**Authors:** James F. Kurose & Keith W. Ross

---

## What Is This Part?

This part follows the Kurose & Ross textbook — the gold standard for learning how the internet actually works. The "top-down" approach means we start where you already live (the web browser, apps, HTTP) and work downward to the physical signals that carry bits across the world.

By the time you finish, you'll understand:

- Why a webpage loads in milliseconds (or doesn't)
- How packets find their way from your laptop to a server in another country
- What TCP actually does behind every API call
- How routers make decisions 100 million times per second
- What SSL/TLS is protecting and how it works

## Why Top-Down?

Most networking courses start at the bottom — with electrons and cables — and work upward. That approach buries you in physical details before you understand why any of it matters.

Kurose & Ross flip the order:

> Start with what you use every day (HTTP, DNS, email), then ask: *what does the layer below need to provide to make this work?*

Each layer is motivated by the one above it. You always know *why* you're learning something before you learn *how* it works.
