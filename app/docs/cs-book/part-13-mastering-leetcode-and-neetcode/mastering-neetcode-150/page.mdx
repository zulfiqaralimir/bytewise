# Mastering LeetCode & NeetCode

## Chapter: Mastering NeetCode 150

---

## What Is the NeetCode 150?

The **NeetCode 150** is a hand-curated list of 150 LeetCode problems selected by [NeetCode](https://neetcode.io) — a software engineer and YouTuber who spent years analyzing what actually shows up in FAANG and top-tier tech interviews.

It is not random. Every problem was chosen because it teaches a **pattern** — a reusable problem-solving technique that recurs across hundreds of interview questions.

> **The core idea:** You don't need to solve 1,000 problems. You need to deeply understand 20 patterns. The NeetCode 150 is the fastest path to that.

---

## Why This List?

- **Breadth without bloat** — covers every major topic that appears in real interviews
- **Pattern-first** — problems are grouped by technique, not by company or difficulty
- **Progression** — within each category, problems escalate in difficulty
- **Community-tested** — used by tens of thousands of engineers who landed offers at Google, Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, and Apple

## How to Use This Chapter

### The Approach That Works

1. **Topic by topic, not random** — do all Sliding Window problems before jumping to Trees
2. **Understand before memorizing** — if you can't explain the pattern out loud, you haven't learned it
3. **Time yourself from week 2 onward** — Easy ≤ 15 min, Medium ≤ 30 min, Hard ≤ 45 min
4. **Review wrong answers immediately** — don't move on until you can re-solve from scratch the next day
5. **Spaced repetition** — revisit each topic every 7–10 days

### What Each Lesson Covers

For every problem in this chapter you will get:

- **Problem statement** — restated in plain English
- **Pattern identification** — which of the 20 patterns this falls under and why
- **Brute force first** — always start naive before optimizing
- **Optimal approach** — step-by-step walkthrough of the efficient solution
- **Complexity analysis** — time and space with explanation
- **Edge cases** — what inputs break naive implementations
- **Code** — clean Python implementation with comments on key lines
- **Variations** — similar problems that use the same pattern

## Prerequisite Knowledge

Before starting, you should be comfortable with:

- **Python or your language of choice** — syntax, loops, functions, classes
- **Big O notation** — understanding O(n), O(n²), O(log n), O(1)
- **Basic data structures** — arrays, hash maps, sets, stacks, queues

If any of those feel shaky, Part 1 (Foundations) covers Big O and data structures from scratch.

## The Interview Reality

Most coding interviews give you:

- **45–60 minutes** for 1–2 problems
- A **medium** as the main question
- A **follow-up** that pushes the solution harder (larger inputs, added constraints)

The NeetCode 150 is designed to make mediums feel routine. If you can solve 80% of this list within time limits, you are interview-ready for senior roles at top companies.

> **Simple takeaway:** Patterns beat memorization. The NeetCode 150 teaches you to recognize what kind of problem you're looking at within 60 seconds — and that skill is what interviewers are actually testing.
